Introduction

Programming has a reputation for being an individual activity. However, it's becoming more and more collaborative. Many companies use Pair Programming to cut down on errors and shorten development time.

What Exactly is Pair Programming?

Pair Programming is when two programmers work together on one piece of code at the same time. Typically one person is the driver and the other is the navigator. The driver is the person who actually types out the code, whereas the Navigator is the person who directs the workflow.

Why Pair Programming is Important to DBC

Pair programming is important to DBC becuase DBC strives to prepare its students for real-world situations. Given the proliferation of pair programming in the work force, DBC places and emphasis on practicing the skill of pair programming. Not only that, pair programming aids in the learning process. It exposes you to different ways of solving problems and helps you overcome hurdles more quickly.
